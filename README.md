# ✈️ HighCloud Airlines Operations Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs end-to-end airline operations analysis (2008–2013) using Excel and SQL to generate actionable business insights related to passenger growth, freight transportation, and operational efficiency.

The objective was to transform raw airline data into structured insights that support data-driven decision-making.

## 📈 Key Business Insights

### 📊 Passenger Growth

- Passenger traffic increased by approximately **24% between 2008 and 2013**.
- The highest growth spike (9% YoY) was observed in 2011, indicating strong post-recession recovery.
- Passenger demand showed consistent upward momentum after 2010.

---

### ✈ Airline Performance

- The top 3 airlines contributed nearly **62% of total passenger traffic**.
- Market share concentration indicates limited dominance by major carriers.
- Mid-sized airlines showed fluctuating performance, suggesting competitive market dynamics.

---

### 📦 Freight Transportation Trends

- Freight volume grew by approximately **31% over the six-year period**.
- Cargo growth accelerated after 2010, aligning with increased trade activity.
- Certain high-density routes contributed disproportionately to total freight movement.

---

### 📉 Operational Efficiency

- Load factor improved from **68% in 2008 to 79% in 2013**, indicating better capacity utilization.
- Route optimization and demand forecasting likely improved operational stability.
- Year-wise volatility reduced after 2010, suggesting improved operational planning.
